Muhammad Ali - The Greatest

Biography

Muhammad Ali (1942-2016) was an American professional boxer, activist, and philanthropist. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest boxers of all time. Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr., he changed his name to Muhammad Ali after converting to Islam.

Achievements

  • Won the Olympic gold medal in boxing in 1960
  • Won the World Heavyweight Championship three times
  • Known for his fights against Joe Frazier, George Foreman, and Sonny Liston
  • Named "Sportsman of the Century" by Sports Illustrated
  • Became an inspirational figure for his activism and refusal to serve in the Vietnam War

Quotes

"Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee."
"I am the greatest. I said that even before I knew I was."
"Service to others is the rent you pay for your room here on earth."
